Welcome to the History of Parkesine!


Alexander Parkes

The Metallurgist and engineer who invented the first man-made plastic called Parkesine.

Alexander Parkes was born on the 29th December 1813 at Suffolk Street, Birmingham, the fourth son of James Mears Parkes and his wife Kerenhappuch Childs.
He died on the 29th June 1890 at the age of 76.


Legacy

Parkes is remembered in several locations:

• A blue plaque was placed on his home in Dulwich, London, in 2002 by the plastics historical society
• In 2004, the birmingham civic society erected a blue plaque commemorating him on the Elkington Silver Electroplating Works
• There is also a plaque on the wall of the site of the Parkesine Works


Elkington Silver Electroplating Works

The Elkington Silver Electroplating Works was a building on Newhall Street in Birminghad, England. It later housed the Birmingham science museum of science and industry until the creation of Thinktank.
